REMEDY WRITING PROCESS +

How we process each case from start to finish

Step 1

Receive Initial Correspondence

Receive initial correspondence from the incarcerated person or family member. We keep diligent logs of all incoming and outgoing correspondence, including scanning all incoming mail.

Step 2

Send New Member Guide

Send the incarcerated person a new member guide with information about how we work, including sign-up and consent forms.

Step 3

Process Sign-up

Receive the sign-up form and any additional information about their complaint. Log this correspondence.

Step 4

Assess Information

A student volunteer and mentor assess whether we have enough information to open a case. If we need more information, we send correspondence to the member to request it. Sometimes we have to go back and forth multiple times.

Step 5

Open Remedy Case

Once we have all the information we need, we open a remedy case and assign it to a student.

Step 6

Research & Summarize

The assigned student advocate reads letters, documents, and correspondence, and summarizes the facts of the case. Sometimes they will need help from a mentor to fully understand the member's situation.

Step 7

Write the Remedy

The student writes the remedy, which includes summarizing the facts, policy research to determine the grounds for relief, an argument about how the grounds were violated, and relief requested.

Step 8

Mentor Review

The remedy is reviewed by a mentor and sent back with edits.

Step 9

Edits

The student incorporates feedback and finalizes the remedy document.

Step 10

Print & Send

The remedy is printed, packaged, rescanned, and mailed out. We copy the initial complaint on a physical form and print five copiesâ€”4 for the prison and 1 for the member. We also print instructions and place post-its to indicate where they should sign and date.

Step 11

Follow-up

We stay in contact with the person through their submittal process and prepare all necessary appeals, repeating steps 3-10.
